Case Study: Spotify


Spotify is a music streaming service available on smartphones, PCs and other devices. In 2024 they had 640 million active monthly users. Most of Spotify’s environmental impact is from energy used in servers and infrastructure for streaming to the customers. Other sources are marketing material, business travel and office space and equipment.



Initiatives


  • Cloud Carbon Footprint plugin for «Backstage», Spotify’s open source developer portal. Provides their development teams with knowledge and tools for green coding. This system is also available for the public.


  • Annual “Hack Week”. Giving the internal developers time and space to create new solutions.


  • Climate handbooks for employees


  • Evaluating the emissions


  • Gathering information on how large the emissions and energy usage of all the different parts in both creating and using the product can help finding what to focus on.


  • Reducing the size of the mobile app and updates


  • Optimizing cloud storage usage


  • Optimizing algorithms to lower the computational load


  • “Work From Anywhere”. Allowing developers to work from home to save real estate footprint.


Results


The footprint is not growing as fast as the user base. Spotify is meeting their sustainability goals.


Conclusion

Spotify is using resources both to find its biggest sustainability issues and to develop solutions and tools to solve them. Streaming hardware usage is the most important contributor to its energy expenditure and optimizing this is the primary focus. Sustainability goals help push the work in the right direction, together with handbooks and software tools.
